<h2 style={{ marginTop: SPACING.px.sectionHeadingTop, marginBottom: SPACING.px.sectionHeadingBottom }}>MAYA - Most Advanced Yet Acceptable</h2>

<SectionHeader
  icon={<Target size={20} />}
  title="UX Philosophy"
  description="Innovate in visuals and behaviors—preserve mental models and task flow."
/>

<div style={{

  <blockquote style={{

  }}>
    "The adult public's taste is not necessarily ready to accept the logical solutions to their requirements if the solution implies too vast a departure from what they have been conditioned into accepting as the norm."
    <footer style={{
      marginTop: SPACING.px.tight,
      fontStyle: 'normal',
      fontSize: TYPOGRAPHY.fontSize.sm,
      color: DEEP_CURRENT[500],
    }}>— Raymond Loewy (Industrial Designer)</footer>
  </blockquote>
</div>

<div style={{
  display: 'grid',
  gridTemplateColumns: 'repeat(2, 1fr)',
  gap: SPACING.px.comfortable,
  marginBottom: SPACING.px.section,
}}>
  <GuidelineCard type="do" title="Innovate in Small Steps">
    Keep core interaction patterns familiar. Modernize progressively—users should immediately understand.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="dont" title="Redesign Everything">
    Avoid radical changes that break muscle memory. Evolution beats revolution.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="do" title="Anchor to Familiar">
    New concepts should use existing mental models. Familiar icons, labels, and layouts reduce friction.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="dont" title="Force New Paradigms">
    Don't make users learn entirely new interaction patterns when established ones work.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="do" title="Respect Conventions">
    Follow platform guidelines, accessibility heuristics, and common UX patterns.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="dont" title="Different for Different's Sake">
    Only break conventions when innovation adds real, measurable value.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="do" title="Modern Visuals, Stable Interaction">
    Update typography, spacing, motion, micro-interactions—while keeping interactions predictable.
  </GuidelineCard>

  <GuidelineCard type="dont" title="Change Learned Behaviors">
    Users have muscle memory. Changing where buttons live or how forms work creates friction.
  </GuidelineCard>
</div>
